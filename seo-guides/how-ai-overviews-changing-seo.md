## TL;DR

- Google's AI Overviews (the AI-generated summaries at the top of search results) now appear on roughly half of US search queries. They are not going away.
- Pages cited inside AI Overviews earn more clicks than standard organic results. Pages that are not cited lose significant click-through rate on affected queries.

Running a 120-person SEO agency means I get the same question from almost every client right now: "Is Google's AI going to kill our organic traffic?"

Short answer: no. Longer answer: it is going to redistribute it. The businesses that understand how AI Overviews work will capture more traffic. The ones that ignore it will lose ground to competitors who adapted.

Google's AI Overviews (previously called SGE, or Search Generative Experience) are the AI-generated summaries that appear at the top of search results. When you search for something like "best CRM for small business," Google now generates a paragraph synthesising information from multiple sources, with citation links to the pages it pulled from.

This is the biggest change to search since featured snippets, and it demands a strategic response. If you are still catching up on the fundamentals, our [beginner's guide to SEO in 2026](/seo-guides/beginners-guide-to-seo-2026/) covers the foundation you will need.

## AI Overviews Are Not Replacing Organic Results

Let me kill the biggest misconception first. AI Overviews sit above the traditional blue links, but they do not replace them. The organic results still exist below the AI summary. Users still scroll. Users still click.

What has changed is the click distribution. Research from multiple sources in early 2026 shows that pages cited within AI Overviews see a meaningful uplift in click-through rate compared to standard position-one results. Being quoted by Google's AI is essentially a super-featured snippet.

The flip side is real too. On queries where AI Overviews appear, pages that are not cited in the summary see noticeable CTR declines. The AI answer satisfies some users entirely, so fewer of them scroll down to the traditional results.

This creates a winner-takes-more dynamic. Getting cited in the AI Overview is now one of the most valuable positions in search. And the pages that get cited share specific characteristics that any business can work toward.

## Google Cites Pages That Already Rank Well

Here is the most important pattern we have observed across our client portfolio: there is a strong overlap between pages that rank in the top organic positions and pages that get cited in AI Overviews.

This makes sense. Google's AI is drawing from its own index. It preferentially cites pages that already demonstrate relevance, authority, and [strong E-E-A-T signals](/seo-guides/eeat-explained/) (that stands for Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness).

That means the fundamentals of SEO have not changed. What has changed is that Google is now rewarding those fundamentals with a new, more prominent placement. If your content already ranks well, you are in a strong position to capture AI Overview citations. If it does not rank well, fixing the basics will improve both your traditional rankings and your AI visibility.

The practical takeaway: do not treat AI Overviews as a separate channel requiring a separate strategy. Treat them as an amplifier of good SEO work you should already be doing.

## Content Structure Is the Differentiator for AI Citations

Not every page that ranks gets cited in AI Overviews. The differentiator, in our experience, is content structure.

AI systems extract content in chunks. They look for clear, self-contained answers under descriptive headings. Pages that bury their key points inside long, meandering paragraphs are harder for the AI to quote, so they get passed over in favour of pages that serve up answers cleanly.

What works:

- **Question-format H2 headings** that match how people actually search. Instead of "Our Approach to CRM Implementation," use "How Long Does CRM Implementation Take?"
- **Direct-answer opening paragraphs.** Start each section with the answer in one to two sentences, then expand with context and detail below.
- **Structured data.** FAQ schema (FAQPage markup), comparison tables, and clearly formatted lists make your content easier for AI to parse and cite.
- **Short paragraphs.** Two to four sentences. Dense walls of text get skipped.

This is the same structure that earns featured snippets, which is not a coincidence. Google's AI is looking for the same qualities: clarity, directness, and authority.

Building content in interconnected clusters rather than isolated posts strengthens the signals that AI systems use to assess your expertise. Our guide on [content hubs and topic clusters](/seo-guides/content-hub-topic-clusters/) breaks down how to structure this.

## AI Mode Is the Next Evolution, and It Favours Brands

Google's AI Mode (launched in early 2026) goes further than AI Overviews. Instead of a summary above the results, AI Mode is a fully conversational search experience where users can ask follow-up questions and get AI-generated responses in a chat-like interface.

For businesses, AI Mode makes brand authority even more critical. In a conversational search flow, the AI needs to decide which sources to trust across multiple rounds of questions. It gravitates toward brands it "recognises" as authoritative in a given space.

This is where [brand mentions across the web become a ranking factor](https://studiohawk.com.au/seo-guides/why-seo-matters-for-ai-search) in a way they never were in traditional search. Being mentioned on industry forums, in YouTube videos, across social media, and in news coverage all contributes to the brand recognition that AI systems use to decide who to cite.

The businesses that will thrive in AI Mode are the ones that exist beyond their own website. If the only place your brand name appears is on your own domain, AI systems have limited signals to assess your authority.

## ChatGPT, Perplexity, and the Multi-Engine Reality

Google is not the only AI search engine that matters. ChatGPT now handles over a billion searches per week. Perplexity is growing rapidly among professionals and researchers. Microsoft Copilot integrates AI answers directly into Bing and the Windows operating system.

Each platform has its own citation logic, but the core principles overlap:

- **ChatGPT** primarily uses Bing's index, which means ranking well in Bing directly improves your chances of being cited by ChatGPT. Most SEO practitioners ignore Bing entirely, which creates opportunity for those who do not.
- **Perplexity** maintains its own crawl infrastructure and tends to cite primary sources and original research heavily. Content with unique data, original analysis, and first-hand experience performs well here.
- **Google AI Overviews** draw from Google's index, so strong Google organic rankings translate directly to AI Overview citations.

The common thread across all of them: original, well-structured content from recognised experts gets cited. Thin, derivative content does not.

## Freshness Matters More Than It Used to

AI systems have a strong recency bias. Research shows the vast majority of pages cited in AI-generated answers have been updated within the past 30 days. Stale content drops out of the citation pool regardless of how strong its other signals are.

This does not mean rewriting everything every month. It means establishing a regular update cycle for your most important pages. Add new data points. Update statistics. Include recent examples. Make sure your schema markup reflects the genuine last-modified date.

For businesses with smaller content footprints, focusing your freshness efforts on your top ten pages by traffic or revenue impact is enough to start.

## FAQ

### Are AI Overviews reducing organic traffic?

It depends on whether your pages are cited. Pages cited inside AI Overviews see higher click-through rates than standard organic results. Pages not cited on affected queries do see CTR declines. The net effect for most well-optimised sites is neutral to positive, because the same content quality that earns good rankings also earns AI citations.

### Can I opt out of AI Overviews?

There is no way to prevent Google from citing your content in AI Overviews while remaining indexed in regular search results. The nosnippet tag removes your site from both snippets and AI summaries, but that would hurt your traditional SEO. For most businesses, being cited in AI Overviews is a net positive.

### Do I need to create different content for AI search?

No. The content that performs well in AI search is the same content that performs well in traditional search, just structured more deliberately. Clear headings, direct answers, original expertise, and up-to-date information. You do not need a separate "AI content strategy." You need a better content strategy overall.

### How do I know if my pages are being cited in AI Overviews?

Search for your target keywords in Google while logged out or in incognito mode. If an AI Overview appears, check whether your site is listed among the citation sources. You can also monitor for traffic changes from queries where AI Overviews are now appearing. Tools like [Search Engine Land](https://searchengineland.com/) regularly publish data on AI Overview prevalence by query type.

### Will AI Overviews expand to more countries?

Yes. Google has been steadily rolling AI Overviews out to more markets throughout 2025 and 2026. They began primarily in the US and have expanded to the UK, Australia, India, and other English-speaking markets. Expect broader international rollout through the rest of 2026, with localised AI Overviews becoming standard across most major markets.
