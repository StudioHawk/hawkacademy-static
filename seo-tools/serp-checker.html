    What this tool is for

    Title tags and meta descriptions don't move rankings on their own, but they decide whether anyone clicks. Use this checker to sanity-check your snippet before you publish — if your title runs past ~600 pixels or your description past ~920 pixels, Google will cut it off mid-sentence or rewrite it. Keep it tight, put the keyword near the front, and give the searcher a clear reason to click.

    Write a better snippet in 5 steps

    1Put your primary keyword in the first 40 characters of the title.
    2Add one concrete differentiator: location, price, speed, brand, or a number.
    3Keep the title under ~600px so the full promise is visible on desktop.
    4Write the description as a benefit + reason to click, not a summary.
    5Stay under ~920px on the description and front-load the value in the first sentence.
