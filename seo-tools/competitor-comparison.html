Why They Rank Above You

Your competitor has 5x more pages, each with 4x more content. Google sees their website as a comprehensive resource and yours as a brochure. They cover 18 topics that customers search for -- you only cover 4. Every missing topic is a search result where they appear and you don't. Their internal linking tells Google which pages matter most, while yours leaves Google guessing.

The 3 Biggest Gaps

Gap #1: Service Pages

What they do:Dedicated page for every service with 800+ words, pricing, and FAQs
What you do:One "Services" page listing everything in bullet points
The impact:They rank for 14 service keywords. You rank for 1.

Gap #2: Trust & Proof

What they do:Team page with bios, reviews on every page, case studies with real results
What you do:Generic about page with no names, no reviews on site
The impact:Google trusts them to give good advice. It doesn't trust you yet.

Gap #3: Location Pages

What they do:Individual pages for 8 suburbs with local content and testimonials
What you do:No location-specific content at all
The impact:They dominate "near me" and suburb searches. You're invisible.

60-Day Catch-Up Plan

Weeks 1-2: Foundation

Expand your top 3 service pages from bullet points to 500+ word pages with pricing, process, and FAQs. Add a proper team/about page with real names and photos.

Weeks 3-4: Trust Signals

Add customer reviews to your homepage and service pages. Create a dedicated testimonials page. Add qualifications and credentials to your team page.

Weeks 5-6: Content Gaps

Build 4 new pages covering your missing high-priority topics. Each page should be 600+ words with internal links to related service pages.

Weeks 7-8: Local & Links

Create 3 location/suburb pages. Build internal links between all pages in logical topic clusters. Add a resource or FAQ section linking everything together.

What You Do Better

Your website loads faster than your competitor's (2.1s vs 4.8s). Page speed matters for both rankings and conversions. That's a real advantage -- make sure every new page you build stays fast by keeping images optimised and avoiding bloated plugins.
